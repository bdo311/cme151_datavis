Hierarchical layouts using more basketball data (2/1/15)

Force chart showing similarity between top NBA players for the 2007-08 season. Data from here. Briefly, for each statistic (PPG, 3PG, BLK, etc) a z-score was calculated for each player compared to all other players on the list, and the Euclidean distance was calculated between each pair of players. All Euclidean distances less than 3 (the most similar) were plotted.

To explore the data a bit more, I applied principal components analysis to the z-score dataset. The first two principal components represent 60% of the variance. Principal component 1 seems to correspond to defense (more negative = better defense).

Principal component 2 seems to correspond to offense (more positive = better offense).
